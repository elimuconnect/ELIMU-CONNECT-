Lesson 1: Physical Properties of Metals

  • Lustrous (shiny when polished)
  • Good conductors (heat and electricity)
  • Malleable (hammered into sheets)
  • Ductile (drawn into wires)
  • Sonorous (rings when struck)

Question: State four physical properties of metals.

Answer: Lustrous, Conductive, Malleable, Ductile

Lesson 2: Composition of Alloys

  • Brass = Copper + Zinc
  • Bronze = Copper + Tin
  • Steel = Iron + Carbon
  • Stainless Steel = Iron + Chromium + Nickel
  • Solder = Tin + Lead

Question: Name three alloys and their composition.

Answer: Brass, Bronze, Steel

Lesson 3: Uses of Metals and Alloys

  • Iron – construction, tools
  • Aluminum – utensils, aircraft
  • Copper – wiring, cooking pots
  • Brass – taps, musical instruments
  • Bronze – medals, statues

Question: State four common uses of metals.

Answer: Iron – tools, Aluminum – utensils, Copper – wires, Brass – instruments

Lesson 4: Rusting of Iron

Rusting is the reaction of iron with water and oxygen.

Conditions:

  • Presence of water
  • Presence of air (oxygen)

Prevention:

  • Painting
  • Greasing
  • Galvanizing
  • Electroplating

Question: How can we prevent rust?

Answer: By coating or protecting metal with other substances
